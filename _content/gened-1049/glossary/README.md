# GENED 1049 Cinematography Glossary

A comprehensive reference guide to cinematography and filmmaking terminology for the East Asian Cinema video production workshop.

## Lighting Concepts

### [Three-Point Lighting](./three-point-lighting.md)
The foundational lighting setup consisting of key light, fill light, and back light working together to create dimensional, professional illumination.

### [Key Light](./key-light.md)
The primary light source that establishes the scene's main illumination, creates the principal shadows, and determines overall lighting direction and mood.

### [Fill Light](./fill-light.md)
Secondary light that softens shadows created by the key light, controlling contrast and mood while maintaining dimensional depth.

### [Back Light](./back-light.md)
Light positioned behind the subject creating a rim or halo effect, separating subject from background and adding three-dimensional depth.

### [Key-to-Fill Ratio](./key-to-fill-ratio.md)
The numerical relationship between key light and fill light brightness, determining the scene's contrast, mood, and visual style. Measured in f-stops.

### [Practical Lighting](./practical-lighting.md)
Light sources visible within the frame (lamps, candles, windows, screens) that serve both as set elements and functional illumination, providing motivated lighting.

## Cinematography & Camera Techniques

### [180-Degree Rule](./180-degree-rule.md)
Fundamental principle of continuity editing maintaining consistent screen direction by keeping the camera on one side of an imaginary axis between subjects.

### [Shot-Reverse-Shot](./shot-reverse-shot.md)
The essential editing pattern for filming conversations, cutting back and forth between characters while maintaining spatial continuity via the 180-degree rule.
